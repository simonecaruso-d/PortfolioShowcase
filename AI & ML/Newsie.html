01 - Problem

Newsletter production is repetitive, research-heavy, and style-inconsistent

Corporate newsletters require topic research, article curation, synthesis, and careful style matching. Teams either spend hours doing it manually or produce generic output that doesn't sound like them

Without Newsie

  • |Research phase: manually browsing news sources, evaluating relevance, copying summaries
  • |Style inconsistency across writers and issues: no systematic way to enforce house tone
  • |AI-written drafts sound generic: no grounding in actual current news or company voice
  • |Revisions require re-prompting from scratch

With Newsie

  • →Style learned from existing PDF newsletters: tone, register, structure, target audience extracted automatically
  • →SerpAPI finds 25 articles; TitleMatcher filters to 5 most relevant for a corporate audience
  • →Full article text scraped and synthesized before writing
  • →Chainlit chat interface: send feedback, get a revised draft instantly without re-running research
  • →Async pipeline: style analysis and news extraction run as separable, composable stages

Key design decision: the article sample is preserved across the review loop. When the user sends feedback to the Reviewer, it receives not just the current draft and the feedback, but also the original article summaries. This means the Reviewer can pull in article details the Writer may have underemphasised

02 - Agent Roster

Six agents, two parallel tracks

Style analysis and news extraction run independently. Their outputs converge at the Writer, which fuses them into a single draft. All agents run async

03 - Pipeline

Topic in, publication-ready draft out

Two parallel tracks converge at the Writer. After first generation, user feedback goes only to the Reviewer

01
Style Track
Learn house voice
  • pdfplumber reads past newsletter PDFs
  • Concatenates all text
  • Analyzer produces style brief
  • Covers tone, structure, lexicon, target
02
News Track
Find relevant articles
  • KeyWordsExtracter generates search query
  • SerpAPI fetches 25 Google News results
  • TitleMatcher picks top 5 titles
  • Priors: business, political, legal themes
03
Scrape + Synthesize
Ground the draft
  • BeautifulSoup scrapes full article body
  • Synthetizer compresses each article
  • 5 summaries built into article context
  • Summaries preserved for Reviewer
04
Write
Produce first draft
  • Writer receives style brief + topic + articles
  • Draft follows analysed structure exactly
  • Sent via Chainlit message
  • Stored as CurrentNewsletter state
05
Review Loop
Conversational refinement
  • User types feedback in Chainlit chat
  • Reviewer applies targeted edits
  • Article context available for grounding
  • Loop continues until user is satisfied

Async design: AutoGen's initiate_chat is synchronous. Wrapping it in asyncio.to_thread lets Chainlit remain responsive while each agent runs: critical because style analysis and multiple article syntheses are serial within their track but the Chainlit event loop must not block
